Kébang — the gathering

Why we exist

Sí:sang Kébang means a gathering, a council — people coming together to decide, to protect, to carry something forward. That is what this movement is: young Mising people gathering, without a headquarters, without a registration number, without a party behind us — working only for our own people.

We watch our language quietly losing ground to borrowed words — not because it is poor, but because it is unused. We are working to reverse that, word by word.

We work as a pressure group, not a political one — raising Mising concerns wherever they need to be raised, and staying accountable only to our community.

What we do

Four threads, one weave

Language Revival

Reviving real Mising words already sitting unused in memory, and proposing native replacements for words we've quietly borrowed from elsewhere.

Educational Awareness

Building understanding of Mising history, identity and language within our own community, especially among the young.

Digitisation of Heritage

Moving Mising oral history, songs, textile patterns and customary knowledge into durable, shareable digital form before they're lost to time.

Research & Documentation

Recording what exists today — words, customs, practices — as a factual record for the generations who come after us.

How we hold ourselves

Principles that don't move

Non-profit

Nothing here is run for profit. Every effort goes back to the community.

Deliberately unregistered

We remain an unregistered, informal collective — light, accountable, and free of institutional weight.

No political affiliation

We are not associated with any political party, and we never will be.

Transparent

What we do and decide is open to the people we work for — our own.

By and for Mising people

Our work is directed at, and accountable to, the Mising community alone.
